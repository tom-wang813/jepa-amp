\subsection{Homology Leakage Audit}
\label{sec:res_homology}

A common confound in AMP benchmarks is high sequence identity between training
and test sets: a model that memorises training peptides can appear to
``transfer'' while merely recalling near-duplicates.
We audit this by filtering each cross-species test set to retain only
peptides with $<80\,\%$ (and separately $<70\,\%$) sequence identity to any
training peptide, using CD-HIT with word size 3.

\centering
\caption{JEPA-AMP zero-shot Pearson $r$ before and after CD-HIT homology
  filtering.
  Values are means over three seeds.
  $n_{\text{filt}}$ reports the mean filtered test-set size.}
\label{tab:homology}
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Route}
  & \textbf{Unfiltered}
  & \multicolumn{2}{c}{\textbf{After filtering}}
  & $n_{\text{filt}}$ ($<$80\,\%) \\
\cmidrule(lr){3-4}
  & & $<$80\,\% ID & $<$70\,\% ID & \\
\midrule
E.~coli $\to$ S.~aureus       & 0.553 & 0.414 & 0.424 & 137 / 722 \\
E.~coli $\to$ P.~aeruginosa   & 0.657 & 0.501 & 0.514 & 82 / 402  \\
S.~aureus $\to$ E.~coli       & 0.384 & 0.324 & 0.299 & 211 / 796 \\
S.~aureus $\to$ P.~aeruginosa & 0.484 & 0.156 & 0.175 & 62 / 402  \\
P.~aeruginosa $\to$ E.~coli   & 0.536 & 0.395 & 0.391 & 355 / 796 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

After filtering, JEPA-AMP retains positive correlation on four of five routes,
confirming that performance is not solely driven by near-duplicate leakage.
The S.~aureus $\to$ P.~aeruginosa route drops to $r = 0.156$ at $<80\,\%$
cutoff (from 0.484 unfiltered), which indicates that the high-identity subset
carries a disproportionate share of the signal on this route and should be
interpreted cautiously.

Filtering to $<70\,\%$ produces results within noise of the $<80\,\%$ values
on four routes (absolute $\Delta \leq 0.021$), suggesting that the
$80\,\%$ threshold already removes the bulk of homologous pairs and tighter
cutoffs reduce $n$ without meaningfully changing conclusions.
Full pairwise BLAST identity matrices are archived in
\texttt{eval\_results/supplementary\_abcd/D\_homology\_leakage/}.

\finding{JEPA-AMP cross-species transfer is partially, but not entirely,
explained by sequence-level homology.
Positive correlations persist at $<80\,\%$ identity on four of five routes;
the S.~aureus $\to$ P.~aeruginosa route is the primary leakage risk and
should be held out from main-text claims.}
