Global activity shifts persist beyond physicochemical conditioning and are
reproduced by the independent ESM-2 scorer (broad-spectrum: $\Delta{=}{-}0.250$
JEPA, $-0.291$ ESM-2; inactivation: $+0.515$ JEPA, $+0.443$ ESM-2).
Species-selective control fails: E.~coli and S.~aureus predicted MIC shift
together under both selective scenarios, and 95\% bootstrap CIs for species
separation include zero.
Generated peptides have 100\% exact-match novelty vs.\ a combined 11{,}299-sequence
AMP reference panel; median nearest-neighbour identity is 0.526.

\finding{MIC conditioning steers global model-predicted activity but cannot separate
species responses.
Both global shifts are reproduced by an independent scorer, reducing single-oracle
circularity; species selectivity is a data limitation (sparse GRAMPA supervision
with dominantly broad-spectrum measurements).}
