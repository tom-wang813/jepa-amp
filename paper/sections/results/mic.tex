\subsection{MIC Prediction}
\label{sec:res_mic}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Formal MIC prediction on GRAMPA test set
  ($\log_2(\text{MIC}/[\mu\text{g\,mL}^{-1}])$ space).
  JEPA runs: $n{=}2{,}057$; ESM-2: $n{=}2{,}059$.
  All artifacts locked in \texttt{checkpoints/formal\_mic\_*}.}
\label{tab:mic}
\begin{tabular}{llcccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{Head} & \textbf{Pearson~$r$} & \textbf{RMSE} & \textbf{MAE} & \textbf{Spearman~$\rho$} \\
\midrule
ESM-2 (35M)           & FiLM-MLP         & 0.554 & 0.635 & 0.495 & 0.530 \\
JEPA-AMP              & FiLM-MLP         & 0.622 & \textbf{0.619} & 0.489 & 0.553 \\
\textbf{JEPA-AMP}     & \textbf{Transformer} & \textbf{0.640} & 0.627 & \textbf{0.488} & \textbf{0.561} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The JEPA Transformer head gives the highest Pearson correlation
($r=\mathbf{0.640}$), and the JEPA FiLM-MLP gives the lowest RMSE
(\textbf{0.619}).
Both heads improve over the ESM-2 baseline by $+0.086$ and $+0.068$
Pearson, respectively.
The RMSE of 0.619--0.627 corresponds to roughly a half-doubling-dilution error
in log$_2$ MIC space.

\finding{AMP-domain JEPA pre-training transfers to MIC prediction, improving over a
general-purpose ESM-2 baseline in correlation and slightly in error.
The gain is moderate and should not be read as assay-ready MIC accuracy.}
