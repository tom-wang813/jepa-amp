\subsection{Conditional Generation: Non-Autoregressive Decoder}
\label{sec:methods_nar}

The \textbf{NAR} decoder replaces autoregressive token generation with
a single bidirectional forward pass.
It shares the same frozen JEPA encoder and adapter as the AR decoder.

\paragraph{Architecture.}
A bidirectional 4-layer Transformer decoder with AdaLN conditioning receives
all-\textsc{mask} tokens of length $L$ and predicts all amino acid positions
simultaneously.
A length prediction head---an MLP applied to mean-pooled $h_\text{ctx}$ and
$\mathbf{c}_\text{emb}$---predicts the target sequence length.

\paragraph{Inference with iterative refinement.}
Starting from all-\textsc{mask} of predicted length, the decoder
(i) predicts all positions in one pass, (ii) re-masks the lowest-confidence
fraction $\rho \cdot L \cdot (1 - s/S)$ of positions, and (iii) repeats for
$S$ refinement steps (default $S=3$, $\rho=0.5$).
This retains the global coherence of bidirectional attention while allowing
iterative improvement of uncertain positions.
