\subsection{Conditional Generation: Masked Diffusion Decoder}
\label{sec:methods_diffusion}

The \textbf{masked diffusion} decoder follows the MDLM absorbing-state
framework~\citep{sahoo2024mdlm,austin2021d3pm}.

\paragraph{Forward process.}
At diffusion time $t \in [0,1]$, each amino acid token is independently
masked (absorbed) with probability $t$.
Special tokens (PAD, BOS, EOS) are never masked.

\paragraph{Backward process.}
A bidirectional 6-layer denoising transformer is trained to predict $x_0$ at
masked positions given the noisy sequence $x_t$ and the conditioning signal:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_\text{diffusion} =
  \mathbb{E}_{t, x_0, x_t}
  \bigl[-\log p_\theta(x_0 \mid x_t,\,\mathbf{c}_\text{emb},\,\tau(t),
  \,h_\text{ctx})\bigr]_{x_t \text{ is masked}},
\end{equation}
where $\tau(t)$ is a sinusoidal timestep embedding
($d_\tau=128$) injected via AdaLN alongside $\mathbf{c}_\text{emb}$.
This is equivalent to a per-position BERT MLM loss with mask rate
proportional to $t$.

\paragraph{Inference.}
Starting from all-\textsc{mask}, the denoiser unmasks
$\lfloor L/T \rfloor$ highest-confidence positions per step for $T_\text{infer}$
steps (default $T_\text{infer}=50$).
