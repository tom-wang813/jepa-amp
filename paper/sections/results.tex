\paragraph{Cross-dataset generalisation.}
On the APD3 independent test set (556 sequences outside any AMPlify split),
JEPA-AMP achieves AUROC~\textbf{0.944} and MCC~\textbf{0.758},
outperforming JEPA-v6 (AUROC~0.880) trained on larger but more diffuse data,
confirming that data quality dominates data quantity for generalisation.

v4 achieves charge $R^2=\mathbf{0.98}$---a \textbf{dramatic improvement} over
v2/v3 ($R^2<0.01$) that suffered from posterior collapse.
All generated sequences are \textbf{100\% novel} (exact-match) with diversity
0.24--0.75 depending on condition, indicating genuine sequence diversity rather
than mode collapse.
AMP classifier scores for cationic conditions (0.36--0.58) are substantially higher
than anionic (0.22--0.25), consistent with the known cationic nature of AMPs and
confirming that conditions induce biologically meaningful sequence changes.

\paragraph{Root cause of v2/v3 failure: posterior collapse.}
v2 and v3 ($R^2<0.01$) produced near-identical sequences regardless of condition.
We trace this to \emph{posterior collapse}: the strong sequence context provided
sufficient information for the decoder, making condition gradients uninformative.
Three mechanisms in v4 jointly resolve this:
(i)~\textbf{physicochemical auxiliary loss} provides direct gradient signal from
sequence composition;
(ii)~\textbf{context dropout} (15\%) forces the decoder to use conditions when context
is absent;
(iii)~\textbf{dual-pathway injection} (AdaLN + cross-attention token) provides
redundant condition access at every decoder layer.

The v5 generator demonstrates strong \textbf{global activity control}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Inactivation} (target $m_i=+3.0$ for all species): actual mean
        $\log_2\text{MIC}=+2.94$, a shift of \textbf{+1.04} log$_2$ units above
        baseline---equivalent to raising the effective MIC from $\approx$3.7 to
        $\approx$7.7~$\mu\text{g\,mL}^{-1}$ ($\times$2.06 fold change).
  \item \textbf{Potentiation} (broad-spectrum, target $-1.0$): actual $+1.48$,
        a shift of \textbf{$-0.42$} log$_2$ units, moving toward lower MIC.
\end{itemize}
Cross-species \emph{selectivity} ($|\Delta_{EC-SA}|<0.10$) remains limited,
reflecting a fundamental data constraint: GRAMPA sequences average only 3.5 of 20
bacterial measurements per peptide, and broad-spectrum activity dominates the
training distribution (only 11.1\% of paired sequences show $|\Delta|>1$).
This is an inherent property of the training data, not an architectural failure.
